Luna the Little Cloud

High above the world, where the wind sang soft songs, lived a little cloud named Luna.

Luna was small. So small she could fit between two sunbeams. So small she cast a shadow no bigger than a butterfly.

But Luna had a dream.

[PAGE TURN]

All around her, the big clouds rumbled and rolled. They made rain that filled rivers. They made thunder that shook the mountains. They made rainbows so bright that children pointed and cheered.

Luna wanted to make rain too.

She puffed herself up. She squeezed her eyes shut. She tried with all her might.

Plip. Plip. Plip.

Three tiny drops. That was all.

[PAGE TURN]

"You're too small," laughed the storm clouds. "Come back when you're bigger."

Luna drifted away, feeling heavy inside even though she was made of mist.

She floated past the mountain peaks. She floated past the tall trees. She floated until she was all alone in a quiet corner of sky.

[PAGE TURN]

That's when she heard it. A tiny sound. A small, sad voice.

"Hello? Is anyone there?" called the flower.

Luna looked down. Far below, in a dusty garden, sat a single flower. Its purple petals drooped. Its leaves curled at the edges, dry and tired.

"I'm so thirsty," whispered the flower. "The big clouds always pass me by. They have important rivers to fill."

Luna floated lower.

"I only have three drops," Luna said. "Is that enough?"

The flower lifted its face toward the sky.

"Three drops," said the flower, "would be everything."

[PAGE TURN]

Luna squeezed herself tight. Tighter than she ever had before.

Plip. Plip. Plip.

Three perfect drops fell through the golden afternoon light. They landed on the flower's petals like tiny jewels.

The flower stretched tall. Its colors blazed bright. "Oh, thank you! Thank you, little cloud!" cried the flower.

[PAGE TURN]

Just then, a small buzz filled the air. A fuzzy bee landed on Luna's edge, panting and tired.

"I've been flying so long," the bee said. "Could you carry me to the meadow? It's just past that hill."

Luna wasn't sure she was strong enough. But she remembered the flower.

"Climb on," Luna said.

[PAGE TURN]

Luna floated over the hill, soft and steady. The bee hummed a happy tune the whole way.

"You're the kindest cloud I've ever met," said the bee, buzzing off toward the wildflowers.

Luna felt warm all over.

[PAGE TURN]

From that day on, Luna found all the tiny things that needed tiny help. A thirsty ladybug. A dusty leaf. A seed waiting to grow.

The big clouds made storms that everyone could see.

But Luna? Luna made small miracles.

And somehow, that felt like the biggest thing of all.

The End.
