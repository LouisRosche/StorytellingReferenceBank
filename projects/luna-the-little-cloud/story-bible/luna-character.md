# Character Sheet: Luna

## Basic Information

**Name**: Luna
**Role**: Protagonist
**Type**: Small cloud (child-analog)
**Age equivalent**: 4-6 years old in cloud terms

## Physical Description

**Appearance**: Very small, wispy cloud. Soft silver-white color with hints of pale lavender at edges. Round, puffy shape but delicate. Can fit "between two sunbeams." Casts a shadow "no bigger than a butterfly."

**Movement**: Floats gently, drifts on wind. When emotional, may sink lower (sadness) or puff up slightly (trying hard). Moves "soft and steady" when carrying the bee.

**Visual cues for illustration**:
- Small compared to other clouds (1/10th their size)
- Soft, rounded edges (not sharp or dramatic)
- Gentle expression (implied through shape)
- Translucent quality—light passes through her

## Psychology

### External Goal
Make rain like the big clouds do.

### Internal Need
To feel valued and useful despite being small.

### Core Fear
Being insignificant. Not mattering.

### Lie She Believes
"I'm too small to help anyone."

### Truth She Learns
Small help matters to those who need it. Being the right size for someone is more important than being the biggest.

## Personality

**Dominant traits**:
- Gentle and kind
- Eager to help
- Sensitive (takes rejection hard)
- Persistent (keeps trying despite setbacks)
- Observant (notices small things others miss)

**Under stress**: Withdraws, floats away to be alone, feels "heavy inside."

**When happy**: Feels "warm all over," floats steadily, extends help readily.

## Voice and Speech

**Vocabulary level**: Simple, age-appropriate

**Speech patterns**:
- Asks questions when uncertain ("Is that enough?")
- Short sentences
- Direct and sincere
- Never boastful

### Dialogue samples
- "I only have three drops. Is that enough?"
- "Climb on."

## Arc

**Starting state**: Sad, rejected, doubting her worth. Trying to be something she's not (a big cloud).

**Key turning points**:
1. Rejection by storm clouds → leaves to be alone
2. Hears the flower's plea → decides to try
3. Success with flower → begins to understand her value
4. Helps the bee → confirms her new understanding

**Ending state**: Confident in her unique value. Actively seeks out small things that need small help. Content with who she is.

## Thematic Function

Luna embodies the message: **Everyone has value, regardless of size.** She represents any child who feels too small, too young, or not enough. Her journey models:
- Finding your unique strength
- The importance of small kindnesses
- Belonging through contribution, not conformity

## Relationships

| Character | Relationship | Dynamic |
|-----------|--------------|---------|
| Storm clouds | Authority figures (rejecting) | Luna wants their approval but doesn't get it; learns she doesn't need it |
| Flower | First friend / recipient of help | Grateful, affirming; validates Luna's worth |
| Bee | Second friend / recipient of help | Reinforces Luna's new understanding |

## Consistency Notes

| Fact | Note |
|------|------|
| Size | Always smallest cloud in any scene |
| Rain | Always exactly three drops—never more |
| Sound | Drops go "Plip. Plip. Plip." (consistent onomatopoeia) |
| Color | Silver-white with lavender hints |
