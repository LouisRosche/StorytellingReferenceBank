# World Document: Luna's Sky

## Core Concept

A sky world where clouds have personalities, feelings, and purpose. Anthropomorphized but not overly magical—clouds do what clouds do (make rain, float, cast shadows), but they experience it emotionally.

## Geography

### The Sky
- Exists "high above the world"
- Wind "sings soft songs" (gentle, musical quality)
- Sunbeams are physical enough for Luna to fit between them
- Has distinct areas: near mountains, above forests, "quiet corners"

### Below
- Mountains with peaks
- Tall trees (forests)
- Gardens with flowers
- Meadows with wildflowers
- Hills

### Spatial relationships
- Luna can see and hear things on the ground
- She can float lower to interact with ground creatures
- Travel takes time but isn't arduous—she "floats" distances

## Cloud Society

### Hierarchy (implied, not stated)
- **Storm clouds**: Big, powerful, respected. Make rain, thunder, rainbows. Have "important" work (filling rivers).
- **Regular clouds**: Not shown, but implied middle tier.
- **Luna**: Smallest. Bottom of hierarchy by size.

### Values
- Size = worth (in conventional view)
- Making big rain = success
- Being noticed = achievement

### Rules
- Clouds make rain—that's their purpose
- Bigger clouds make more rain
- Small clouds are dismissed ("Come back when you're bigger")

## What Luna Disrupts

Luna's arc challenges the cloud society's values:
- Small contributions matter
- Being "the right size" for someone is success
- Noticing the overlooked is valuable

## Tone

**Gentle**: No villains. Storm clouds aren't cruel—just dismissive. The world isn't hostile, just indifferent to small things.

**Hopeful**: Problems have solutions. Kindness is rewarded emotionally (feeling "warm all over").

**Sensory-rich**: Golden afternoon light, soft wind songs, purple petals, fuzzy bees. The world is beautiful and tactile.

## Supporting Characters

### The Flower
- Single purple flower in a dusty garden
- Overlooked by big clouds (too small to matter to them)
- Speaks softly, whispers
- Grateful and affirming
- Represents those who need small help

### The Bee
- Fuzzy, tired from flying
- Needs a ride, not rain
- Hums happy tunes when helped
- Calls Luna "the kindest cloud"
- Represents varied needs (not just water)

### Storm Clouds
- Plural, unnamed
- Rumble, roll, laugh
- Not malicious—just focused on big things
- Represent conventional success/authority

## Things Not In This World

- Human characters
- Explicit magic (clouds talk but don't cast spells)
- Danger or threat
- Death or serious harm
- Complex technology
- Time pressure or urgency

## Sensory Palette

| Sense | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **Sight** | Silver-white clouds, golden light, purple petals, green leaves, blue sky |
| **Sound** | Wind singing, plip-plip-plip raindrops, bee buzzing, thunder rumbling |
| **Touch** | Soft mist, wispy edges, fuzzy bee |
| **Implied** | Warmth of sun, cool of mist, dryness of dusty garden |

## Illustration Notes

- Sky should feel vast but not intimidating
- Luna should be visible but clearly small
- Warm color palette (golds, soft blues, lavenders)
- Ground scenes show intimate scale (single flower, one bee)
- Storm clouds are big but not scary (rounded shapes, no menacing features)
