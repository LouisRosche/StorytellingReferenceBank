# Eleanor Marchetti (nee Callahan)

## Basic Information
- **Age**: 65
- **Role**: Eldest sibling, de facto family leader
- **Residence**: Camden, Maine (40 minutes from Port Clyde)
- **Occupation**: Retired school administrator
- **Marriage**: Ray Marchetti, 42 years. Two adult sons (neither present in story).

## Physical Description
- Tall, straight-backed, carries herself with deliberate dignity
- Silver hair kept short and practical
- Reading glasses on a chain, constantly adjusting them
- Hands never still - folding, sorting, organizing
- Dressed in good wool, muted colors, nothing frivolous
- Face: handsome rather than pretty, deep lines, her mother's jaw

## Psychology

### Surface Presentation
- Competent, organized, in control
- The one who handles things
- Somewhat imperious; expects deference
- Impatient with inefficiency and emotion

### Hidden Self
- Exhausted by the performance of competence
- Deeply resentful of siblings' freedom while she carried the burden
- Terrified of what happens when she stops being needed
- Has built her entire identity around being "the responsible one"

### Wound
- At 22, Eleanor gave up a fellowship to Yale to help care for her father during his first illness. Her mother praised her sacrifice. Her siblings went on to live their lives.
- She never recovered from understanding she was the expendable one - the one who could be asked to stay.

### Want vs. Need
- **Want**: To be acknowledged as the one who earned Ruth's trust, who was there, who matters most
- **Need**: To release the identity she built around sacrifice, to find out who she is without the burden

## Speech Patterns

### Distinctive Elements
- Speaks in complete, grammatically correct sentences
- Uses proper names, not nicknames
- Tends toward the imperative: "We need to..." "You should..."
- When stressed, becomes more clipped, more formal
- Rarely asks questions - makes statements
- Interrupts, but precisely, at natural breaks

### Examples
- "The caterer needs a headcount by tomorrow. I've assumed forty. If that's wrong, tell me now."
- "Marcus, that's not helpful."
- "I'm not asking what you feel about it. I'm telling you what needs to happen."

### When Breaking Down
- Sentences fragment
- Repetition
- Voice rises in pitch before cracking
- "You don't understand. You weren't here. You have no idea—"

## Relationships

### With Ruth
- Complicated. Dutiful love, buried resentment.
- Eleanor was Ruth's caretaker, not confidante.
- Believed she and Ruth had an unspoken understanding. The revelation destroys this.

### With Marcus
- Old rivalry. He escaped; she stayed.
- She resents his freedom; he resents her superiority.
- Neither has spoken honestly to the other in decades.

### With Helen
- Exasperation. Helen's emotions seem indulgent to Eleanor.
- But also protection - Helen is still "the baby."
- A grudging affection neither would admit.

### With Ray
- Ray is her anchor. But she keeps him at arm's length emotionally.
- He knows her better than she realizes. She needs him more than she admits.

### With Sophie
- Fond but formal. The grandmother-adjacent relationship.
- Sees Sophie as an observer, doesn't take her seriously as an adult.
- This will shift.

## Arc in the Story

**Beginning State**: In control, managing the aftermath, brittle beneath the surface.

**Breaking Point**: Learning Ruth confided in Pastor Oakes, in Sarah - in everyone except Eleanor. The realization that her sacrifice earned her nothing.

**Transformation**: She must accept that control was always an illusion. Ruth was unknowable not because Eleanor failed but because people are unknowable. The choice at the end - to invite Sarah - is Eleanor choosing truth over the comfortable narrative.

**End State**: Still wounded, still processing, but no longer clenching. She touches Ruth's pillow. She allows herself to not understand.
