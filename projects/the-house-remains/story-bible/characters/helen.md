# Helen Callahan

## Basic Information
- **Age**: 52
- **Role**: Youngest sibling
- **Residence**: Burlington, Vermont
- **Occupation**: Fiber artist (weaving, textiles), teaches workshops
- **Marriage**: Never married. Sophie's father was a brief relationship; he's uninvolved.

## Physical Description
- Small, slight, moves with nervous energy
- Long graying hair, usually loose or in a messy braid
- Dresses in layers, natural fibers, colors
- Hands always touching things - fabric, hair, face
- Face: Ruth's face twenty years younger, except the expressions are more mobile
- Often looks like she's about to cry, or just finished

## Psychology

### Surface Presentation
- Emotional, expressive, "the sensitive one"
- Believes in spiritual connection, art as truth
- The one who voices what others won't
- Sometimes exhausting, sometimes essential

### Hidden Self
- Uses emotionality to avoid action
- Believed she had a special connection with Ruth that exempted her from the practical work
- Afraid her "artist's soul" is self-indulgence
- Has her own secrets she hasn't shared with Sophie

### Wound
- As the youngest, Helen never had to do anything hard. She was protected, excused, "fragile."
- She internalized this as identity but suspects she could have done more.
- When Ruth got sick, Helen couldn't handle it. Eleanor stepped in (as always). Helen felt relief and shame in equal measure.

### Want vs. Need
- **Want**: To feel Ruth's presence spiritually, to process this through emotion, to find meaning
- **Need**: To accept that her "special connection" with Ruth was partly self-constructed, that love requires presence as well as feeling

## Speech Patterns

### Distinctive Elements
- Speaks in rushes, incomplete thoughts
- Interrupts herself
- Emotionally responsive - volume and pace vary with feeling
- Uses sensory language: "I can feel..." "It's like..."
- Rhetorical questions to herself: "Do you know what I mean? I don't know if I mean it."
- When upset, sentences fragment, restart, fragment again

### Examples
- "I just - she was always - I can't believe she never told me. I would have understood. I would have."
- "It's not about the box, Marcus. It's about... it's the idea of the box. What she felt she had to hide."
- "I need air. I can't be in this house right now."

### When Centered
- Slower, clearer, capable of genuine insight
- "Maybe we all have boxes. Maybe we're just surprised hers was literal."

## Relationships

### With Ruth
- Believed they had a soul-deep connection.
- Ruth encouraged this - Helen was her "sensitive" one.
- But Ruth kept Sarah from Helen just like the others. The special connection was a story.

### With Eleanor
- Complicated. Eleanor protected and resented her.
- Helen feels guilty for not helping more with Ruth, defensive about it.
- They circle each other with affection they can't express cleanly.

### With Marcus
- Closest sibling relationship.
- They both escaped in different ways - he literally, she internally.
- He's gentle with her; she can read him.

### With Sophie
- Close but complicated. Sophie is becoming an adult Helen doesn't fully recognize.
- Helen kept Sophie's father's identity vague. There's a conversation coming.
- She sees herself in Sophie and worries.

### With Ray
- Fond. He's the calm in Eleanor's storm.
- He's kind to her in a way her own father wasn't.

## Arc in the Story

**Beginning State**: Arriving already emotional, expecting to process this through feeling, assuming her connection with Ruth will carry her through.

**Breaking Point**: Ruth kept Sarah secret from Helen too. The "special connection" was a story Helen told herself.

**Transformation**: Helen must reckon with the difference between feeling close and being close. By the end, she tells Sophie one of her own secrets - takes an action, not just an emotion.

**End State**: Still emotional, still Helen, but with more humility. She understands love is not mystical - it's showing up.

## Voice Persona Notes (for TTS)
- Higher register, variable pace
- Emotional responsiveness should be audible - not histrionic, but present
- Slight Vermont earthiness
- When upset: rushed, fragmentary, breathless
- When centered: surprising clarity
- Should sound like someone who feels everything and wishes she didn't
