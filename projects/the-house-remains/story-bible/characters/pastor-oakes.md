# Pastor David Oakes

## Basic Information
- **Age**: 73
- **Role**: Local minister, inadvertent truth-teller
- **Residence**: Port Clyde, Maine (45 years)
- **Occupation**: Methodist minister, semi-retired
- **Marriage**: Widowed 8 years. Three grown children elsewhere.

## Physical Description
- Thin, angular, slightly stooped
- White hair, closely trimmed
- Clerical collar only for services; usually in simple button-down
- Hands that shake slightly - essential tremor, nothing serious
- Face: kind, lined, eyes that have heard too much confession
- Moves carefully - age and bad knees

## Psychology

### Surface Presentation
- Pastoral calm, professional comfort with grief
- The voice of the institution, of community
- Speaks in homilies and gentle guidance
- Present without demanding

### Hidden Self
- Carries the secrets of half the town
- Ruth's secret weighed on him for years
- He's not sure he believes anymore, but he believes in the practice
- Ready to retire but doesn't know who he'd be without the collar

### Wound
- His wife died without warning. Heart attack.
- He officiated at her funeral, held it together, and something broke inside.
- He envies people who can fall apart.

### Want vs. Need
- **Want**: To help the family through the funeral with grace
- **Need**: To confess his own failure - he kept Ruth's secret when she asked, but he also let it slip on purpose. He couldn't simply tell them, so he engineered the revelation.

## Speech Patterns

### Distinctive Elements
- Slow, deliberate, weight behind each word
- Often pauses before responding - thinking or praying, unclear
- Uses scripture rhythms without quoting scripture
- Comfortable with silence in ways that make others uncomfortable
- Speaks around things first, then directly

### Examples
- "Your mother was... a woman of depths. Not all of them easy."
- "I imagine you have questions. I can answer some of them."
- "Forgiveness is not about the person forgiven. It's about the person forgiving. You'll discover that when you're ready."
- "I spoke carelessly. That was intentional. I'm sorry."

### When Confessing
- Simpler syntax
- Eye contact held
- "She asked me to tell you, if something sudden happened. I couldn't do it directly. I'm a coward in that way."

## Relationships

### With Ruth
- Confidante and pastor
- She told him about Sarah thirty years ago
- He urged her to tell the children; she refused
- He kept her secret; it cost him something

### With the Siblings
- Knew them all as children
- Baptized Sophie
- They respect him without warmth - he represents institution, obligation

### With the Town
- Knows everyone's business
- Trusted to keep it
- Ready to be done but doesn't know how to stop

## Arc in the Story

**Beginning State**: Arrives to discuss funeral arrangements, a professional doing his job.

**Breaking Point**: Reveals more than he meant to. Watches the family fracture.

**Transformation**: Confesses to Eleanor that his "slip" was semi-intentional. He gives himself up to her judgment.

**End State**: Officiates the funeral that includes Sarah. Speaks honestly about Ruth's complexity. His best sermon in years.
