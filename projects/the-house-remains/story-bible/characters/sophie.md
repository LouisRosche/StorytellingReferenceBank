# Sophie Marchetti-Callahan

## Basic Information
- **Age**: 25
- **Role**: Helen's daughter, grandchild generation
- **Residence**: Portland, Maine (recently moved from Burlington)
- **Occupation**: Environmental nonprofit, communications role
- **Relationship**: Single, recently ended a long relationship

## Physical Description
- Helen's face with more composure
- Dark hair, usually pulled back
- Practical style - flannel, jeans, good boots
- Tall for the family, takes after unknown father
- Eyes that miss nothing, often looking slightly elsewhere
- Quiet body - doesn't fidget like Helen

## Psychology

### Surface Presentation
- Quiet, observant, helpful
- The peacemaker, the one who gets coffee
- Seems mature for her age
- Present without claiming space

### Hidden Self
- Acutely aware of family dynamics she's not supposed to see
- Doesn't know who her father is - Helen has evaded for years
- Uses helpfulness to avoid dealing with her own stuff
- Just ended a relationship because she felt herself becoming her mother

### Wound
- Growing up with Helen meant absorbing instability as normal.
- Sophie became the adult in the relationship early.
- She doesn't trust emotional volatility - including her own.

### Want vs. Need
- **Want**: To support her family, to understand death, to be useful
- **Need**: To accept that adulthood means living with uncertainty, that the adults never had answers either

## Speech Patterns

### Distinctive Elements
- Speaks less than others; chooses words carefully
- When she speaks, it matters
- Asks the questions others avoid
- No regional accent - moved around too much
- Uses "right?" as a softener, seeking confirmation
- Sometimes trails off when she realizes no one's listening

### Examples
- "Does anyone want more coffee?"
- "I just... did any of you actually know her? Like, really know her?"
- "Mom. That's not fair to Aunt Eleanor."
- "She must have been so lonely. Keeping all of this."

### When Something Lands
- Goes quieter, not louder
- Shorter sentences
- Needs to leave the room to process

## Relationships

### With Helen
- Love and exhaustion
- She's been parenting her mother for years
- Recently creating distance, which feels like betrayal
- Wants to know who her father is; afraid to destabilize Helen by asking

### With Eleanor
- Respects her even while seeing the brittleness
- Eleanor treats Sophie like a helpful child; Sophie wishes she'd see her as an adult
- By the end, Eleanor will.

### With Marcus
- Drawn to him - another observer
- He's gentle with her in ways he isn't with others
- She sees his loneliness; it mirrors something in her

### With Ray
- Easy warmth
- He's the closest thing to a stable grandfather figure
- They work well together on practical tasks

### With Ruth
- Loved her grandmother but knew her through Helen's lens
- Now questioning everything she thought she knew
- This is her first death as an adult - the first time she's seeing grief up close

## Arc in the Story

**Beginning State**: In support mode, watching, helping, not claiming space.

**Breaking Point**: Watching the adults crumble. Realizing they have no more answers than she does.

**Transformation**: She stops expecting adults to be adults. She finds Ruth's final letter - becomes an active participant, not just observer.

**End State**: More comfortable with uncertainty. Decides to ask Helen about her father - not now, but soon.
