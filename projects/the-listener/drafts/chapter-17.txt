He had a plan for what came after. He had been building it since before the first session—possibly since long before that. She had been inside his plan from the beginning, positioned exactly where he'd wanted her.

She thought: then the plan can be used. Whatever shape he's built, I can learn it. And then I can use the shape against him.

She turned off the desk lamp. Picked up her bag. Left the note on the desk—she would not carry it home.

Outside, the February night was clear and very cold. She walked to her car thinking about Maya. About the four calls. About what it meant that he had been at Emma's school before he ever sat down across from her and said he was having intrusive thoughts.

The question was not whether she was inside his plan. She was.

The question was whether she could learn it faster than it closed around her.
