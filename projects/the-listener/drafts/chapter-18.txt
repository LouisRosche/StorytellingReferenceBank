[NARRATOR]
The letter came to the practice on a Friday morning, addressed to Dr. Sarah Mercer in the same precise handwriting as the note. Cream envelope, no return address. Carla brought it in with the mail and set it on the desk and didn't comment because she'd been working for Sarah for six years and understood the difference between information she was meant to have and information she was meant to process and set down.

Sarah waited until Carla had left the room to open it.

---

*Dr. Mercer,*

*I have been thinking about what I said at our last session. I am aware that it may have felt abrupt—that the transition from one mode of conversation to another, without mutual agreement, is somewhat discourteous. I hope you'll accept this letter as an acknowledgment of that.*

*The sessions have been valuable to me. I mean that genuinely, and I mean it more broadly than the obvious irony might suggest. I have not spoken about these things with anyone in ten years. Speaking has cost something, and I find I don't regret the cost.*

*What I am proposing is not an end. I think we have both recognized, in different ways, that there is more to say. What I am proposing is a different setting for the remainder of the conversation—one that doesn't carry the formal constraints we've both been navigating. I would like to meet with you as one adult to another, outside any professional context. I have a great deal to tell you. I think some of what I have to tell you would be—not comforting, exactly. But clarifying.*

*I know what you've been trying to do. I've known since October. I want you to understand that I don't experience this as a betrayal. You love your sister. I loved her also, in my own way, and that "in my own way" has always been the crux of the disagreement.*

*I will not contact Emma or your husband. That was a message, as you understood it to be, and the message has been received. I have no interest in further distressing you through them. My interest is in the conversation we haven't finished.*

*I'm proposing dinner. Somewhere public—you may choose the location. I understand that public space will feel safer. I have no objection to it.*

*I'll leave the decision in your hands.*

*Yours,*
*T.*

---

[NARRATOR]
She read it twice. Then set it flat on the desk and looked at it.

The structure of it was familiar—she recognized the rhetorical moves from the sessions. The acknowledgment of her perspective followed by the reframe. The distinction between *in my own way* and the way that didn't require explanation. The gesture of concession—*you may choose the location, I understand that public space will feel safer*—that positioned him as the reasonable party.

And: *I know what you've been trying to do. I've known since October.*

October. Two months into their sessions. Which meant he had been performing openness and partial disclosure while fully aware that she was performing clinical neutrality. Two people performing their respective roles for the benefit of—what? Each other? The recorder? Some audience neither of them had named?

*I don't experience this as a betrayal.*

She thought about that. About what it meant to be someone who loved your sister's murderer with enough detachment to parse the ethics of his own surveillance operation.

She picked up her phone and called Cole.

She hung up. The letter was still flat on the desk.

She thought about what Thomas had said at the end of the last session: *we can do better.* She had understood it as a threat. Re-reading the letter now, she wondered if it was also something else—if, in the specific psychology of Thomas Vane, there was a version of this that wasn't a threat but a genuine proposal. If he had built, in the years since Maya, a story in which he and Sarah were two people who understood something together that nobody else could understand, and this dinner was the conclusion of that story.

If he believed that. If he'd been working toward this meeting the way he'd worked toward Maya—with a patience that was, in its own way, incomprehensible.

She thought: what does he want from me, if not what he did to Maya?

She thought: he wants to be received. He wants someone who knows what he did to look at him and not look away.

He'd said as much, in session. *What would it mean to be understood. It would mean someone acknowledging that what I felt was real.*

She thought: I can give him the shape of that. One more time. In a public place of my choosing.

And she could be recording every word.

She folded the letter back into its envelope. Put it in the locked drawer.

She had until Tuesday to decide whether the woman she was becoming was one she would recognize afterward.
