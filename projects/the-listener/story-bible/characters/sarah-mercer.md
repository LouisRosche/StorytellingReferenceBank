# Character Sheet: Dr. Sarah Mercer

## Basic Information

**Name**: Dr. Sarah Mercer (nee Aldridge)
**Role**: Protagonist
**Age**: 38
**Gender**: Female
**Occupation**: Licensed Clinical Psychologist, specializing in trauma therapy

## Physical Description

**Appearance**: 5'7", slender but not fragile. Chestnut hair worn in a low bun during work, loose at home. Gray-green eyes that hold steady when listening. Early lines at the corners - she looks tired even when she isn't. A small scar on her left wrist from a childhood accident she never discusses.

**Clothing style**: Work: muted professional. Charcoal slacks, cream blouses, one good blazer. No jewelry except wedding ring and small pearl earrings (Maya's). Home: soft sweaters, bare feet, Owen's stolen sweatshirt.

**Physical mannerisms**: Still when listening - unnaturally still. Tilts head slightly to the right. Hands always visible, palms often open. When stressed: touches the scar on her wrist, tucks hair behind ear even when it's pinned.

**Voice**: Alto, measured pace. Never rushes. Speaks in complete sentences even casually. Professional voice is warmer than personal voice - irony she doesn't notice.

## Psychology

### External Goal
To protect her family from Thomas Vane. To see him brought to justice for Maya's murder.

### Internal Need
To forgive herself for failing Maya. To allow herself to be vulnerable and imperfect.

### Core Fear
That she is fundamentally cold. That her inability to help Maya wasn't negligence but nature.

### Core Wound
Ignored Maya's calls the night she died. Has never fully acknowledged this to anyone, including herself.

### Lie She Believes
"If I stay in control, nothing bad can happen." (Corollary: "Maya died because I lost control of my attention for one night.")

### Truth She'll Learn
Control is an illusion. Vulnerability is not weakness. Forgiving herself is not the same as excusing herself.

## Personality

**Dominant traits**: Observant, composed, intelligent, protective, emotionally guarded

**Contradictions**: Professionally encourages vulnerability, personally avoids it. Listens for a living but doesn't hear her own needs. Helps others process trauma while burying her own.

**How she sees herself**: Competent. Responsible. Necessary.

**How others see her**: Calm, reliable, slightly intimidating, hard to know.

**Under stress, she**: Becomes quieter. Sleeps less. Cleans obsessively. Stops eating full meals. Drinks wine alone.

## Voice and Speech

**Vocabulary level**: Educated (clinical precision available but rarely used in personal contexts)

**Sentence structure**: Complete sentences, measured. Rarely interrupts. Questions are genuine, not rhetorical.

**Speech patterns**:
- Uses "I notice..." and "I wonder if..." professionally
- Rarely uses contractions when stressed
- Never curses (except once, at climax - carries weight)
- Deflects with questions when personal topics arise

**Topics she returns to**: Work, Emma, the weather, what Owen needs

**Topics she avoids**: Maya, her parents, the night it happened, how she's really feeling

### Dialogue Sample

```
[SARAH]: "You seem agitated today. Would you like to talk about what's bringing that up?"

[SARAH]: "I don't think that's relevant right now."

[SARAH]: "Owen, I'm fine. It was just a long day. How was yours?"

[SARAH]: "She was my sister. That's all you need to know."
```

## Background

**Formative experiences**:
- Raised by emotionally unavailable parents who prized composure
- Learned early: feelings are weakness, analysis is safety
- Maya was the emotional one, Sarah the "responsible" one
- Maya's death froze Sarah at 28 emotionally

**Family/Origin**: Parents deceased (mother cancer, father heart attack - both since Maya's death). No other siblings. Married into Owen's large, warm family. Finds them overwhelming.

**Education**: BA Psychology (UC Berkeley), PhD Clinical Psychology (University of Washington), post-doctoral specialization in trauma (private practice)

**Skills/Expertise**:
- Therapeutic listening and intervention
- Micro-expression recognition
- Emotional regulation (her own and others')
- Research methodology
- Compartmentalization (professional asset, personal liability)

## Arc

**Starting state**: Controlled, composed, secretly fragile. Believes she's healed from Maya's death. In reality, has entombed her grief.

**Key turning points**:
1. Recognizes Thomas - control begins cracking
2. Thomas reveals he knows she knows - facade shatters
3. Owen learns the truth about the calls - last wall falls
4. Chooses to save Thomas's life - defines who she will be

**Ending state**: Integrated. Vulnerable but strong. In her own therapy. Able to hold complexity - she failed, she's also worthy of forgiveness.
