# Character Sheet: Thomas Vane

## Basic Information

**Name**: Thomas Vane
**Role**: Antagonist
**Age**: 42
**Gender**: Male
**Occupation**: Architect (partner at Whitmore & Associates)

## Physical Description

**Appearance**: 6'1", lean, well-maintained. Dark hair going silver at the temples, worn neatly parted. Pale blue eyes that hold contact a beat too long. Clean-shaven, angular face. Handsome in a cold way - like a building. No distinguishing marks. Forgettable until you notice him noticing you.

**Clothing style**: Precise. Tailored suits in navy or charcoal. White shirts always. No tie unless required. Expensive watch (Patek Philippe - noticed, never mentioned). Shoes polished. Everything fits perfectly.

**Physical mannerisms**: Economical movement. Sits perfectly still when listening. Enters rooms quietly. Stands closer than comfortable. Blinks rarely. When pleased: slight lift at corner of mouth, not quite a smile.

**Voice**: Baritone, soft-spoken. Forces you to lean in. Never raises volume, even in conflict. Pauses before answering - not thinking, savoring.

## Psychology

### External Goal
To be truly understood by Sarah. To complete the "connection" he believes he had with Maya through her sister.

### Internal Need
None he can access. His pathology prevents genuine insight. (What he *actually* needs: to not exist in this form. But that's not achievable within the story.)

### Core Fear
Being ordinary. Invisible. Unremarkable.

### Core Wound
Childhood emotional neglect. Mother was physically present, psychologically absent. Father left when Thomas was 7. Thomas learned: to be seen, you must *make* people see you.

### Lie He Believes
"Love is attention. If I watch someone completely enough, if I know them fully, they will have to love me."

### Truth He'll Learn
Nothing. His arc is exposure, not growth. The moment Sarah "understands" him is also the moment she rejects him - the connection was always illusion.

## Personality

**Dominant traits**: Intelligent, patient, controlled, obsessive, performative

**Contradictions**: Presents as empathetic, lacks empathy. Seeks connection through violation. Believes he loves while treating love objects as possessions.

**How he sees himself**: Misunderstood romantic. More sensitive than others. A man who loves too deeply.

**How others see him**: Colleagues: brilliant, aloof, difficult. Clients: charming, attentive. Victims: (after the fact) "There was always something off, but I couldn't name it."

**Under stress, he**: Becomes more controlled, not less. Voice gets softer. Movements become slower. The pressure is internal, not visible.

## Voice and Speech

**Vocabulary level**: Erudite (uses it to intimidate and impress)

**Sentence structure**: Complete, formal. Rarely uses contractions. Grammatically precise. Questions are often rhetorical.

**Speech patterns**:
- Pauses before answering - not hesitation, power
- Uses names often: "Sarah" repeated feels like touch
- Employs passive voice to avoid ownership: "Mistakes were made"
- Never says "I'm sorry" - says "That's unfortunate" or "I understand how you might feel that way"
- Vocabulary shifts to match the listener (code-switching as manipulation)

**Topics he returns to**: Beauty in precision, the nature of attention, being misunderstood

**Topics he avoids**: Direct questions about Maya (deflects until ready), his childhood, why he's really in therapy

### Dialogue Sample

```
[THOMAS]: "I appreciate that question, Sarah. Most people don't ask the real questions."

[THOMAS]: "Loneliness isn't about being alone. It's about being unseen. Wouldn't you agree?"

[THOMAS]: "She didn't understand what I was offering. I've come to terms with that."

[THOMAS]: "You're very observant. It's one of the things I noticed about you. Right away."
```

## Background

**Formative experiences**:
- Age 7: Father leaves without goodbye. Thomas watches him pack the car.
- Age 12: Begins "collecting" - photos, objects, information about classmates
- Age 23: First serious "interest" (a coworker). Ends when she moves cities.
- Age 30: Meets Maya. Convinces himself this is different.
- Age 32: Maya dies. Thomas feels loss, not guilt.
- Age 32-42: Watches Sarah. Waits. Plans.

**Family/Origin**: Single mother, working-class, emotionally absent (depressive). No siblings. Father abandoned family, never made contact.

**Education**: Architecture degree (Princeton), thesis on "surveillance and sacred space." Summa cum laude.

**Skills/Expertise**:
- Architectural design (genuinely talented)
- Pattern recognition
- Patience (pathological)
- Social mimicry
- Technical surveillance

## Arc

**Starting state**: In control. Executing a decade-long plan. Confident he will achieve "connection."

**Key turning points**:
1. Sarah's recognition - the game becomes explicit (he welcomes this)
2. Sarah's fear satisfies him less than expected - he needs more
3. Sarah's "understanding" in final session - he believes he's won
4. Sarah's rejection after understanding - his entire framework collapses
5. Arrest - not the ending he wanted, but still: he's seen

**Ending state**: Exposed, pathetic, still deluded. In custody, he writes Sarah letters she doesn't read. He never stops believing they had something real.
