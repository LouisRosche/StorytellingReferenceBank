# The Listener - World Bible

## Setting Overview

**Primary Location**: Pacific Northwest suburban/urban (unnamed city modeled on Portland/Seattle blend)
**Time Period**: Present day
**Season**: Late autumn into winter (November - February)
**Atmosphere**: Grey, rain-soaked, intimate interiors against cold exteriors

---

## Key Locations

### Sarah's Therapy Practice - Westlake Professional Building

**Physical Description**:
- Third floor of a renovated 1920s office building
- Exposed brick, hardwood floors, original crown molding
- Large windows overlooking a tree-lined street (now bare branches)
- Waiting room: comfortable but not cozy. Professional calm.
- Sarah's office: two leather chairs angled toward each other, no desk between. Bookshelf wall. One plant. One painting (abstract, calming blues).
- Sound: Excellent soundproofing. White noise machine outside door. The silence is intentional.

**Atmosphere**: Controlled, liminal space. Neither home nor institution. A place for revealing what's hidden.

**Sensory Details**:
- Smell: Subtle diffuser (eucalyptus), coffee from down the hall
- Sound: Radiator clanks in winter, distant traffic, the white noise hush
- Light: Adjustable lamps, never overhead fluorescent. Sarah controls the lighting.

**Key Scenes Here**: All therapy sessions with Thomas, the climactic final session

---

### The Mercer Home

**Physical Description**:
- Craftsman bungalow in established neighborhood
- Three bedrooms, home office Sarah never uses
- Kitchen is the heart: farmhouse sink, always dishes in the rack
- Living room: comfortable couch, family photos everywhere except Maya
- Master bedroom: Owen's side cluttered, Sarah's minimalist
- Basement: Owen's workshop, Emma's abandoned playroom

**Atmosphere**: Warm surfaces, cool undercurrents. A family performing "normal."

**Sensory Details**:
- Smell: Owen's coffee (always fresh), Emma's fruit shampoo, old wood
- Sound: Neighborhood dogs, school bus at 7:45, Owen's NPR
- Light: Morning sun through east windows, evening lamps too bright

**Key Scenes Here**: Sarah's unraveling, Owen confrontations, final image peace

---

### Thomas Vane's Apartment

**Physical Description** (Sarah never enters; described by Cole):
- High-rise downtown, 14th floor
- All white: white walls, white furniture, white kitchen
- Immaculate. Nothing personal visible.
- One room locked. Cole never saw inside.
- Large windows facing west - toward the suburbs. Toward Sarah.

**Atmosphere**: Operating theater. Predator's cave with no warmth.

**Sensory Details** (imagined/described):
- Smell: Nothing. Aggressively neutral.
- Sound: Climate control hum. City muffled by glass.
- Light: Too much. Nowhere to hide.

---

### Detective Cole's "Office" - The Back Booth at Hal's Diner

**Physical Description**:
- All-night diner, downtown edges near the precinct
- Cracked vinyl booths, Formica tables
- Cole's booth: back corner, facing both doors
- Corkboard behind the counter cluttered with local flyers
- Coffee always fresh, always bitter

**Atmosphere**: Noir melancholy. A man stuck in the past.

**Sensory Details**:
- Smell: Bacon grease, burnt coffee, rain on wool coats
- Sound: Griddle sizzle, bell on door, oldies radio
- Light: Harsh fluorescent tempered by neon window signs

**Key Scenes Here**: Sarah-Cole meetings, exposition dumps, ethical compromises

---

### Maya's Grave - Evergreen Memorial

**Physical Description**:
- Section 7, row 14. Simple headstone.
- Old cemetery, mature trees, winding paths
- Bench nearby (Sarah never sits)
- In November: leaves covering everything. Groundskeeper always behind.

**Atmosphere**: Quiet, unresolved. Sarah comes but doesn't speak.

**Sensory Details**:
- Smell: Wet earth, decaying leaves, distant wood smoke
- Sound: Wind through trees, distant highway, crows
- Light: Grey. Always grey when Sarah visits.

**Key Scenes Here**: Sarah's single visit during story, internal monologue

## Temporal Elements

### Timeline of Key Past Events

**12 years before story**:
- Maya meets "a guy" at a bookstore. Starts dating casually.
- Relationship ends after 3 months. Maya breaks it off.
- The watching begins.

**11 years before story**:
- Maya notices things: car in her parking lot, items moved in apartment
- Reports to police. Nothing actionable.
- Sarah dismisses Maya's fears. "You're being dramatic."

**10 years before story** (November):
- November 15: Maya calls Sarah four times between 10 PM and midnight
- Sarah sees calls, silences phone. Tired of "crisis mode."
- November 16, 2 AM: Maya dies. Officially ruled home invasion.
- Thomas Vane interviewed as "acquaintance." Released.
- Case goes cold within six months.

**7 years before story**:
- Sarah completes trauma therapy specialization
- Marries Owen
- Never discusses Maya's death in detail with him

**5 years before story**:
- Emma born
- Sarah believes she's moved on

**2 years before story**:
- Detective Cole retires
- Continues investigating Maya's case privately
- Identifies Thomas Vane as prime suspect, no evidence

**Present day**:
- Thomas Vane appears in Sarah's practice
- Story begins

## Social Context

### Sarah's Professional World
- Well-respected in trauma therapy circles
- Colleagues: cordial but not close
- Supervisor Dr. Ruth Henley: 30-year friendship with Sarah's mother (deceased)
- Practice: three other therapists share the floor, minimal interaction

### Thomas's Professional World
- Architect at prestigious firm
- Known as "brilliant but difficult"
- No close friends (by design)
- Clients love him - charming when needed
- Coworkers uneasy but can't articulate why

### Owen's World
- High school English teacher
- Beloved by students, respected by colleagues
- Coaches debate team
- Comes from large, functional family (contrast to Sarah)

---

## Technology and Communication

### Surveillance Fears
- Ring doorbells: everywhere in the neighborhood. Thomas could be watching through any of them.
- Cell phones: Thomas's recording of the family proves he's close
- Social media: Emma has Instagram. Sarah monitors it obsessively.
- Professional: Therapy sessions not recorded by default. Sarah considers starting.

### Communication Patterns
- Sarah: Texts sparse, calls rarely. Prefers in-person.
- Thomas: Perfect grammar, formal. Eerily polite.
- Owen: Texts constantly, emoji-heavy, voice notes.
- Cole: Phone calls only. Doesn't trust texts.

## Atmospheric Touchstones

### Weather as Mood
- Story begins in late November: bare branches, early dark, constant drizzle
- Deepens into December: first frost, Christmas pressure, darker earlier
- Climax in January: coldest days, snow threatens but doesn't fall
- Resolution in February: first buds, grey becoming silver

### Sound Palette
- White noise machines (therapy)
- Rain on windows (everywhere)
- Radiator clank (Sarah's office, Cole's diner)
- Footsteps on wet pavement (Thomas following)
- Silence (what Sarah fears most)

### Visual Palette
- Greys: concrete, sky, Sarah's wardrobe
- Warm woods: her office, her home, the book in her hands
- White: Thomas's apartment, hospital, blank pages
- Red: one accent per chapter - Emma's coat, Maya's necklace, blood on a knife

## Rules of This World

1. **Police are ineffective** - Not corrupt, just limited. Thomas operates in gaps.

2. **Therapy is sacred and vulnerable** - The setting matters. Intimacy is weapon.

3. **Everyone is watching** - Surveillance culture means privacy is illusory.

4. **The past is never past** - Unprocessed trauma recurs until addressed.

5. **Words are the only weapons** - No guns until Cole offers one. The violence is verbal, psychological. The knife appears only at climax.

6. **Weather matches mood** - Not subtle. Rain when Sarah cries, cold when Thomas speaks.

7. **Names matter** - Thomas Vane (vane = weathercock, but also vain). Sarah = princess, but also "barren" in Hebrew. Maya = illusion.
