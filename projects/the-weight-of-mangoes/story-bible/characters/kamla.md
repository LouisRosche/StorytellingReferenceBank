# Character Sheet: Kamla

## Basic Information

**Name**: Kamla Morrison (nee Persaud)
**Role**: Protagonist (ensemble)
**Age**: 58
**Gender**: Female
**Occupation**: Hospital administrator at Brooklyn Hospital Center

## Physical Description

**Appearance**: Taller than her mother—five-six. Medium brown skin, lighter than Nani, darker than Sunil. Hair black with grey streaks she's stopped dyeing. Worn natural now, shoulder-length, often pulled back. Full-figured in a way she's stopped apologizing for. Her mother's eyes but her father's mouth.

**Clothing style**: Professional during the week—blazers, slacks, sensible heels. On weekends, jeans and tunic tops. Always earrings (different every day), always lipstick (even to the grocery store).

**Physical mannerisms**: Talks with her hands. Touches people when she speaks—arm, shoulder. Stands with arms crossed when guarding herself. Sighs audibly when frustrated, then catches herself.

**Voice**: Clear, projects well—hospital administrator voice. Caribbean rhythm emerges when relaxed or stressed. Laughs loud and suddenly. Can cut with a sentence.

## Psychology

### External Goal
To hold the family together for her mother. To make this birthday perfect.

### Internal Need
To be seen not as the bridge but as someone who also needs crossing.

### Core Fear
That she has lost herself in service. That her mother doesn't see how much she's given up.

### Core Wound
She was ten when they emigrated. Old enough to remember Trinidad vividly, young enough to assimilate. She's spent her life between: never fully Caribbean, never fully American. Neither home is really home.

### Lie They Believe
That holding things together is her purpose. That needing help is failure.

### Truth They'll Learn
That her mother sees more than she says. That asking for what she needs is not betrayal.

## Personality

**Dominant traits**: Competent, warm, controlling, anxious, generous, proud

**Contradictions**: Capable but resentful of her capability being assumed. Loving but keeping score. Holds family together while sometimes wishing they'd fall apart just so she could rest.

**How they see themselves**: The responsible one. The one who stayed. Tired but irreplaceable.

**How others see them**: The organizer. The one to call. Intimidating when angry. Safe when you need her.

**Under stress, they**: Over-organize. Snaps at people, then apologizes. Cleans aggressively.

## Voice and Speech

**Vocabulary level**: Educated, professional, code-switches fluidly.

**Sentence structure**: Complex when calm, fragments when stressed.

**Speech patterns**:
- Caribbean emerges under pressure: "What stupidness is this?"
- Uses full names when annoyed: "Sunil Persaud, I am talking to you"
- "Lord have mercy" (from Michael, originally Jamaican)
- Rhetorical questions when frustrated: "You think I don't have things to do?"

**Topics they return to**: Logistics, schedules, whether people are taking care of themselves

**Topics they avoid**: Her own needs, her marriage's challenges, whether she resents her siblings

### Dialogue Sample

```
[KAMLA]: I've been here since six AM. Six. Where was everybody else?

[KAMLA]: Ma, sit down. I have this. I always have this.

[KAMLA]: You know what, never mind. Never mind. I'll do it myself.

[KAMLA]: Don't tell me about sacrifice. I'm the one who—no, you know what? Not today. Not today.
```

## Background

**Formative experiences**:
- Ten years old on the plane from Trinidad—remembers the altitude pressure, her ears popping, her father saying "Don't look back"
- Her father's death—she was 32, had to be strong
- Her wedding—a negotiation between Caribbean and American expectations

**Family/Origin**: Born in Debe, raised partly there, partly in Brooklyn. The transition child.

**Education**: Public school in Brooklyn, community college, bachelor's degree at night while working, master's in healthcare administration at 45.

**Skills/Expertise**: Managing people. Managing crises. Cooking (her mother's recipes, close but not exact). Forgiveness (eventually).

## Arc

**Starting state**: Exhausted but performing. Holding resentment she can't express.

**Key turning points**:
1. Argues with Anita about pushing Nani—realizes she's the one who wanted the stories
2. Hears her mother's history—sees her differently
3. Final scene alone with Nani—finally asks for what she needs

**Ending state**: Still the bridge, but no longer alone on it. Has received something from her mother she didn't know she needed.
