# NGSS Standards Reference: Grades 5–12

Missouri Learning Standards for Science (MLS-S) are Missouri's adoption of the Next Generation Science Standards (NGSS). This reference covers all grade bands for a 5–12 school, organized for rapid story-planning lookup.

---

## The Three Dimensions

Every NGSS performance expectation integrates all three dimensions simultaneously. Stories that hit a standard should engage all three, not just the content.

### Dimension 1 — Science and Engineering Practices (SEPs)

What scientists and engineers *actually do*. These are the narrative engine: the doing-science moments that drive scenes.

| # | Practice | Story Moment It Generates |
|---|----------|--------------------------|
| 1 | **Asking Questions / Defining Problems** | The inciting moment — scientist confronts something that doesn't fit |
| 2 | **Developing and Using Models** | The creative leap — scientist visualizes what can't yet be seen |
| 3 | **Planning and Carrying Out Investigations** | The methodical grind — design, failure, revision, persistence |
| 4 | **Analyzing and Interpreting Data** | The revelation — pattern emerges from noise; scientist sees something |
| 5 | **Using Mathematics and Computational Thinking** | The precision — quantifying, predicting, modeling with numbers |
| 6 | **Constructing Explanations / Designing Solutions** | The synthesis — scientist assembles evidence into coherent claim |
| 7 | **Engaging in Argument from Evidence** | The conflict — peer review, dismissal, scientific community pushback |
| 8 | **Obtaining, Evaluating, and Communicating Information** | The legacy — scientist reads predecessors, publishes, is heard or ignored |

**Narrative note**: SEP 7 is where most scientist stories stall. The argument from evidence — the moment the community resists — is where character is revealed. Don't skip it.

### Dimension 3 — Crosscutting Concepts (CCCs)

Big ideas that appear across all science. These are the thematic throughlines that give a story resonance beyond the specific content.

| # | Concept | Thematic Power |
|---|---------|---------------|
| 1 | **Patterns** | Recognition vs. explanation — seeing isn't understanding |
| 2 | **Cause and Effect** | Mechanism and accountability — why things happen matters |
| 3 | **Scale, Proportion, and Quantity** | The humbling of zoom — what's invisible at one scale is obvious at another |
| 4 | **Systems and System Models** | Interconnection — you can't change one thing |
| 5 | **Energy and Matter** | Conservation and transformation — nothing is lost, only moved |
| 6 | **Structure and Function** | Form follows purpose — what something looks like tells you what it does |
| 7 | **Stability and Change** | Equilibrium and disruption — what holds, what breaks, what transforms |

## Missouri Learning Standards Alignment

Missouri adopted NGSS in 2016 as the Missouri Learning Standards for Science (MLS-S). The performance expectations are identical; Missouri additionally emphasizes:

- **Missouri context in ESS standards**: Mississippi River basin, Ozark geology, Missouri River hydrology, Bootheel agricultural soils
- **Local hazard mapping**: earthquake risk in the New Madrid Seismic Zone (one of the highest-risk zones in North America — directly relevant to STL)
- **Environmental history**: Meramec River flooding, air quality in St. Louis, lead contamination in North St. Louis neighborhoods (ESS3.C, LS1 health implications)
