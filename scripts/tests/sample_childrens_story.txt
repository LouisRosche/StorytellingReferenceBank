Luna the Little Cloud

Luna was the smallest cloud in the whole wide sky.

All the big clouds made rain. They made thunder. They made rainbows that stretched from mountain to sea.

But Luna was too small. When she tried to rain, only three drops fell. Plip. Plip. Plip.

"You're too tiny," said the storm clouds. "Come back when you're bigger."

Luna floated away, feeling sad.

Then she saw something below. A tiny flower, all alone in a dry garden. Its petals drooped. Its leaves curled.

"Please," whispered the flower. "I only need a little drink."

Luna smiled. She squeezed herself tight.

Plip. Plip. Plip.

[PAGE TURN]

Three perfect drops landed on the flower's petals.

The flower stretched tall. Its colors grew bright. "Thank you, little cloud! That was exactly enough!"

Luna realized something important. She wasn't too small. She was just the right size for someone who needed her.

[PAGE TURN]

From that day on, Luna found all the tiny things that needed tiny help. A thirsty ladybug. A dusty leaf. A seed waiting to grow.

The big clouds made storms.

But Luna? Luna made small miracles.

The End.
