The Little Seed

Once upon a time, in a garden full of sunshine, there lived a very small seed named Pip.

Pip was round and brown and no bigger than a freckle. She sat in the dark earth and wondered what she would become.

[PAGE TURN]

"Will I be a tall tree?" Pip asked the earthworm who wriggled past.

"Maybe," said the earthworm. "Or maybe something else entirely."

Pip felt the rain seep down through the soil. She drank it up. Every single drop.

[PAGE TURN]

Days passed. Pip felt something stirring inside her. A tiny green shoot pushed upward, reaching for the light.

Up through the darkness. Up through the crumble of soil. Up, up, up.

And then — sunlight.

[PAGE TURN]

"Look at you!" said the earthworm. "You're growing."

And she was. Each day Pip stretched a little taller. Her stem grew strong. Her leaves unfurled like tiny green hands reaching for the sun.

Then one morning, a bud formed at her very top. It swelled. It turned golden. And then it opened.

Pip was a sunflower.

[PAGE TURN]

"You see?" said the earthworm. "You were something wonderful all along."

Pip swayed in the breeze, happy and tall and bright. And every morning, she turned her face to the east and waited for the sun to rise.

Because that's what sunflowers do.

The End.
