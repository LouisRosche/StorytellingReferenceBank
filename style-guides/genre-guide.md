Craft and TTS notes by genre.

## Literary Fiction

**Defining characteristic**: Prose style and thematic depth over plot mechanics.

### Craft Priorities

| Priority | Application |
|----------|-------------|
| **Sentence-level craft** | Every word choice intentional |
| **Interiority** | Deep access to character consciousness |
| **Ambiguity** | Meaning emerges; not explained |
| **Subtext** | What's unsaid carries weight |
| **Thematic resonance** | Plot serves theme, not reverse |

### Structure Notes

- Often rejects conventional plot structures
- Scene-Sequel works well (internal processing matters)
- Rasa theory applicable (emotion as goal)
- Circular narratives common

### TTS Considerations

**Narrator style**: Measured, intelligent, intimate
- Mid-Atlantic or neutral accent for accessibility
- Slower pace allows prose appreciation
- Emotional subtlety > dramatic range
- Pauses matter: leave space for reflection

**Example persona prompt**:
> "Narrator in late forties, gender-neutral presentation. Thoughtful, unhurried pace. Voice suggests someone reading to a single person in a quiet room. Intelligent but never condescending. Finds the music in sentences."

### Common Word Counts

- Short story: 2,000-7,500
- Novella: 17,500-40,000
- Novel: 70,000-100,000

## Thriller/Suspense

**Defining characteristic**: Sustained tension through withholding and revelation.

### Craft Priorities

| Priority | Application |
|----------|-------------|
| **Pacing** | Relentless forward momentum |
| **Information control** | Reader knows just enough to worry |
| **Stakes escalation** | Each chapter raises the cost of failure |
| **Ticking clock** | Deadline pressure |
| **Twists** | Earned reversals, not cheap shocks |

### Structure Notes

- Three-Act with accelerating Act 2
- Jo-ha-kyū essential (rapid kyū resolution)
- Scene-Sequel ratio: 4:1 (minimal processing time)
- Save the Cat's "All Is Lost" beat critical

### Subgenres

| Subgenre | Focus | Pacing |
|----------|-------|--------|
| **Psychological** | Internal threat, unreliable narrator | Slower, dread-building |
| **Action** | Physical threat, chase/escape | Fast, kinetic |
| **Legal/Medical** | Procedural, expertise | Medium, detail-heavy |
| **Domestic** | Family/relationship threat | Slow burn, intimate |
| **Techno** | Technology threat | Medium, exposition-heavy |

### TTS Considerations

**Narrator style**: Controlled tension, driving energy
- Clear diction (fast pace must remain intelligible)
- Vary pace: slow for dread, fast for action
- Cliffhanger chapter endings need pause before next chapter
- Character voices should be quickly distinguishable

**Example persona prompt**:
> "Male narrator, mid-thirties, American neutral. Clear and precise diction. Controlled energy with tension underneath. Pace varies: measured for suspense, quickens for action. Never melodramatic—lets the text do the work."

### Common Word Counts

- Novel: 70,000-90,000
- Series entry: 80,000-100,000

## Romance

**Defining characteristic**: Central love story with emotionally satisfying ending.

### Craft Priorities

| Priority | Application |
|----------|-------------|
| **Chemistry** | Readers must feel the attraction |
| **Emotional beats** | Meet-cute, first kiss, dark moment, HEA/HFN |
| **Character growth** | Love catalyzes transformation |
| **Tension** | Obstacles (external and internal) to union |
| **Sensory detail** | Physical and emotional presence |

### Structure Notes

- Two protagonists, dual POV common
- Save the Cat works well (clear beat sheet exists for romance)
- Rasa: Shringara (love) as dominant, supported by others
- "Dark moment" (Act 2 end) is critical beat

### Heat Levels

| Level | Content | TTS Notes |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| **Sweet** | Closed-door, minimal physical | Standard narration |
| **Sensual** | Emotional + some physical | Slightly slower intimate scenes |
| **Steamy** | Explicit scenes | Consider narrator comfort, pacing |
| **Erotic** | Frequent explicit content | Specialized narrator needed |

### TTS Considerations

**Narrator style**: Warm, emotionally present
- Dual narrators for dual POV can work well
- Intimate scenes need slower pace, breathy quality
- Dialogue-heavy: character voice distinction matters
- Emotional range: vulnerable, playful, passionate, tender

**Example persona prompt**:
> "Female narrator, early thirties. Warm and emotionally present. Natural smile in the voice. Can shift from playful banter to quiet vulnerability. For intimate moments: slower, more breathy, deeply felt. Never reads sex scenes mechanically."

### Common Word Counts

- Category romance: 50,000-65,000
- Single title: 75,000-100,000
- Romantic suspense: 80,000-100,000

## Fantasy

**Defining characteristic**: Secondary world or magical elements integral to story.

### Craft Priorities

| Priority | Application |
|----------|-------------|
| **Worldbuilding** | Coherent, immersive, not info-dumpy |
| **Magic system** | Consistent rules (hard) or mysterious (soft) |
| **Stakes** | Personal + world-level |
| **Naming** | Pronounceable, memorable, consistent |
| **Scope management** | Epic ≠ bloated |

### Structure Notes

- Three-Act scales to epic length
- Hero's Journey common (but not required)
- Kishotenketsu for slice-of-life fantasy
- Series arc vs. book arc planning essential

### Subgenres

| Subgenre | Focus | Typical Length |
|----------|-------|----------------|
| **Epic** | World-spanning, multiple POV | 100,000-200,000+ |
| **Urban** | Magic in contemporary world | 80,000-100,000 |
| **Cozy** | Low stakes, comfort | 60,000-80,000 |
| **Dark** | Grimdark, morally gray | 100,000-150,000 |
| **Romantasy** | Romance + fantasy blend | 90,000-120,000 |
| **LitRPG** | Game mechanics, progression | 80,000-120,000 |

### TTS Considerations

**Narrator style**: Versatile, able to handle names and multiple characters
- Pronunciation guide essential (include in production notes)
- Character voice differentiation critical for large casts
- Action scenes need clear spatial choreography in narration
- Worldbuilding passages: engaged, not encyclopedic

**Example persona prompt**:
> "Narrator with theatrical range. British or mid-Atlantic accent. Comfortable with unusual names and terminology. Can voice multiple distinct characters (vary pitch, pace, accent for each). Epic without being pompous. Action scenes: crisp and spatial. Quiet moments: intimate."

### Common Word Counts

- Standalone: 90,000-120,000
- Series entry: 100,000-150,000
- Epic: 150,000-250,000+

## Science Fiction

**Defining characteristic**: Speculative technology or science drives the narrative.

### Craft Priorities

| Priority | Application |
|----------|-------------|
| **Plausibility** | Science grounded in logic (even if extrapolated) |
| **Extrapolation** | "If this, then what?" |
| **Novum** | The new thing that makes it SF |
| **Human stakes** | Technology serves character story |
| **Exposition balance** | Teach without lecturing |

### Structure Notes

- Three-Act common
- Mystery structure works for many SF plots
- Jo-ha-kyū for hard SF action sequences
- Often thesis-driven (explore an idea through story)

### Subgenres

| Subgenre | Focus | Tone |
|----------|-------|------|
| **Hard SF** | Scientific accuracy | Technical, precise |
| **Space opera** | Adventure, scale | Epic, dramatic |
| **Cyberpunk** | Tech + society, near-future | Gritty, noir |
| **Military SF** | Combat, hierarchy | Direct, tactical |
| **Cozy SF** | Optimistic, character-driven | Warm, hopeful |
| **Climate fiction** | Environmental futures | Urgent, often literary |

### TTS Considerations

**Narrator style**: Intelligent, clear on technical content
- Pronunciation of technical terms, ship names, etc.
- Exposition-heavy sections: maintain engagement
- Alien/AI characters: decide on voice approach (human? synthetic?)
- Pacing varies by subgenre (military = fast; hard SF = measured)

**Example persona prompt**:
> "Narrator with clear, intelligent delivery. American neutral or slight British. Comfortable with technical terminology without sounding robotic. For AI characters: subtle synthetic quality. For aliens: decisions documented in speaker map. Maintains human warmth even in clinical settings."

### Common Word Counts

- Short SF: 2,000-7,500 (strong tradition)
- Novel: 80,000-110,000
- Space opera: 100,000-150,000

## Mystery/Crime

**Defining characteristic**: Puzzle to solve, truth to uncover.

### Craft Priorities

| Priority | Application |
|----------|-------------|
| **Fair play** | Clues available to reader |
| **Misdirection** | Red herrings, false leads |
| **Pacing** | Revelation rhythm |
| **Detective competence** | Protagonist earns solution |
| **Stakes** | Justice, truth, or survival |

### Structure Notes

- Often uses specific mystery structures (setup, investigation, revelation)
- Information control is everything
- Scene-Sequel with emphasis on detective's processing
- Climax = revelation (may precede action climax)

### Subgenres

| Subgenre | Tone | Violence Level |
|----------|------|----------------|
| **Cozy** | Light, community-based | Off-page |
| **Traditional** | Fair-play puzzle | Minimal |
| **Hardboiled** | Noir, cynical | Moderate |
| **Police procedural** | Realistic, detailed | Varies |
| **Domestic noir** | Psychological, intimate | Psychological |
| **Thriller-mystery** | Suspense + puzzle | Can be high |

### TTS Considerations

**Narrator style**: Depends heavily on subgenre
- Cozy: Warm, engaged, community feel
- Hardboiled: World-weary, sardonic
- Procedural: Professional, precise
- Dialogue-heavy genre: character voices essential

**Example persona prompt (hardboiled)**:
> "Male narrator, fifties. World-weary but not defeated. Slight gravel in the voice. Sardonic, finds dark humor. Pace: unhurried, deliberate. For dialogue: distinct character voices, period-appropriate if historical."

### Common Word Counts

- Cozy: 65,000-80,000
- Traditional: 70,000-90,000
- Thriller-mystery: 80,000-100,000

## Horror

**Defining characteristic**: Designed to evoke fear, dread, or revulsion.

### Craft Priorities

| Priority | Application |
|----------|-------------|
| **Atmosphere** | Dread before horror |
| **Pacing** | Slow build, sharp release |
| **Vulnerability** | Characters must be at risk |
| **The unknown** | Fear of what might be |
| **Restraint** | What you don't show matters |

### Structure Notes

- Jo-ha-kyū critical (slow jo, building ha, explosive kyū)
- Rasa: Bhayanaka (terror) or Bibhatsa (disgust)
- Scene-Sequel: extended sequels build dread
- Often subverts save-the-cat expectations

### Subgenres

| Subgenre | Fear Type | Examples |
|----------|-----------|----------|
| **Gothic** | Atmosphere, decay | Haunted houses, family secrets |
| **Cosmic** | Existential, unknowable | Lovecraftian, scale horror |
| **Body** | Physical violation | Transformation, infection |
| **Psychological** | Mind, perception | Unreliable reality |
| **Quiet** | Subtle, creeping | Literary horror |
| **Splatter** | Graphic violence | Gore-forward |

### TTS Considerations

**Narrator style**: Controlled, building
- Pacing is everything: slow build, don't rush the scare
- Atmosphere through voice (lower, more resonant in tension)
- Quiet moments need silence (longer pauses)
- Avoid melodrama: understatement often scarier

**Example persona prompt**:
> "Narrator with control and restraint. Gender-neutral, mid-range pitch. Pace: deliberately slow during tension, quickens at release. Voice drops slightly lower during dread. Pauses are pregnant, not empty. Never screams—suggests horror, doesn't perform it."

### Common Word Counts

- Short horror: 2,000-7,500 (strong tradition)
- Novel: 70,000-90,000
- Epic horror: 100,000-150,000

## Memoir/Personal Non-Fiction

**Defining characteristic**: True story, author's experience, crafted for meaning.

### Craft Priorities

| Priority | Application |
|----------|-------------|
| **Truth** | Emotional truth even when memory uncertain |
| **Reflection** | Past self vs. narrating self |
| **Theme** | What does this experience mean? |
| **Scene craft** | Show, don't summarize |
| **Vulnerability** | Reader must feel the stakes were real |

### Structure Notes

- Often chronological but not required
- Thematic organization can work
- Circular narrative common (return to beginning, transformed)
- Rasa applicable (what should reader feel?)

### TTS Considerations

**Narrator style**: Usually author-read, but if not:
- Match author's voice/persona from interviews if available
- Intimate, reflective quality
- Regional accent if relevant to story
- Emotional authenticity > polish

**Example persona prompt**:
> "Narrator reading as if this is their own story. Reflective, sometimes pausing as if remembering. Emotional moments: present but not performed. Regional accent if the memoir is place-based. Feels like sitting with someone telling you their life."

### Common Word Counts

- Essay collection: 50,000-70,000
- Memoir: 70,000-90,000

## Narrative Non-Fiction

**Defining characteristic**: True events told with fiction's techniques.

### Craft Priorities

| Priority | Application |
|----------|-------------|
| **Research** | Accuracy is non-negotiable |
| **Scene reconstruction** | Show events, don't summarize |
| **Character** | Real people with arc and interiority |
| **Narrative drive** | Story structure despite factual constraints |
| **Transparency** | Reader knows what's documented vs. inferred |

### TTS Considerations

**Narrator style**: Authoritative but engaging
- Clear, intelligent delivery
- Comfortable with names, dates, technical content
- Maintains narrative energy through exposition
- Distinct voices for quoted individuals

**Example persona prompt**:
> "Narrator with journalistic authority and storyteller's warmth. American neutral. Clear on facts without being dry. Quoted dialogue: slight characterization without caricature. Maintains reader's sense that this really happened."

### Common Word Counts

- Long-form journalism: 10,000-30,000
- Book: 80,000-120,000

## Self-Help/Prescriptive Non-Fiction

**Defining characteristic**: Reader transformation through actionable advice.

### Craft Priorities

| Priority | Application |
|----------|-------------|
| **Clarity** | Ideas must be immediately graspable |
| **Structure** | Logical progression, easy navigation |
| **Examples** | Abstract → concrete |
| **Action** | What should reader *do*? |
| **Authority** | Why should reader trust you? |

### TTS Considerations

**Narrator style**: Warm authority
- Encouraging without being saccharine
- Clear on instructions/steps
- Slower on key concepts
- Direct address to listener

**Example persona prompt**:
> "Narrator with warm authority. Like a trusted mentor. Clear, encouraging, never condescending. Slows slightly for key concepts. Direct address feels natural: 'You might notice...' or 'Try this...' Genuine belief in reader's capacity to change."

### Common Word Counts

- Short guide: 20,000-40,000
- Standard: 50,000-70,000

## Middle Grade (MG)

**Defining characteristic**: The protagonist discovers how the wider world works beyond family. The core question is belonging: where do I fit?

### Craft Priorities

- **Protagonist age**: 10-13. Readers read up, not down — the protagonist should be at the upper end of the target audience age or slightly above.
- **Voice**: Clear, immediate, and grounded in the character's specific world. Interior life is present but less self-analytical than YA. MG characters experience feelings intensely but don't always have language for them.
- **Stakes**: The world is expanding. Problems feel enormous because they are being encountered for the first time — first betrayal, first moral complexity, first awareness of injustice. Respect the scale without condescending.
- **Content boundaries**: No graphic violence, explicit sexuality, or sustained despair without hope. Characters can face darkness, but the narrative provides a path through it. This is not censorship — it's audience-appropriate craft.
- **Humor**: MG rewards humor more than any other category. Even serious MG benefits from moments of levity. The humor comes from character voice and situation, not from ironic distance.
- **Adults**: Present but not in control. Adults may be absent, ineffective, or part of the problem — but avoid the trope of universally incompetent adults. One trustworthy adult who nonetheless cannot solve the protagonist's problem is more realistic than a world of oblivious parents.

### Structure Notes

- **Pacing**: Faster than literary fiction, but MG can be more episodic than YA. Chapter length typically 1,500-3,000 words.
- **Scene-Sequel ratio**: 2:1 (more scene than sequel). MG readers want forward motion; extended reflection works only in measured doses.
- **Framework**: Three-Act and Save the Cat both work well. Kishotenketsu suits quieter MG (slice-of-life, literary MG).

### TTS Notes

- **Narrator persona**: Warm but not saccharine. Clear articulation. Energy that matches the protagonist's engagement with the world.
- **Pace**: Slightly faster than adult literary; slightly slower than children's picture book.
- **Emotional register**: Wide range, but the baseline is engaged curiosity rather than anxiety or gravity.

### Word Count

- Chapter books (ages 7-10): 15,000-25,000
- Standard MG: 25,000-50,000
- Upper MG / MG fantasy: 50,000-75,000

## Young Adult (YA)

**Defining characteristic**: Identity formation drives the narrative; protagonist agency and emotional intensity are non-negotiable.

### Craft Priorities

| Priority | Application |
|----------|-------------|
| **Protagonist agency** | Characters drive the story — choices matter, have consequences, and are respected by the narrative |
| **Identity as theme** | External plot is a vehicle for "who am I becoming?" — the internal arc is the real story |
| **Emotional intensity** | Stakes feel total; YA readers experience emotions fully, not ironically — do not soften them |
| **Voice specificity** | Narrator voice must be distinctive, credible, and non-condescending; YA lives and dies here |
| **Authentic relationships** | Friendships, family dynamics, and first romantic relationships must feel real, not convenient |
| **Earned clarity** | YA doesn't mean simplified — characters should be complex but not obscured |

### Structure Notes

- Three-Act works well with strong protagonist-driven beats
- Save the Cat's B-story (friendship/mentor) is almost always present and weighted heavily
- Identity arc runs parallel to plot arc; they must converge at the climax
- Series common: manage standalone arc and series arc simultaneously

### Subgenres

| Subgenre | Focus | Notes |
|----------|-------|-------|
| **Contemporary** | Real-world identity, relationships | Voice and emotional authenticity paramount |
| **Fantasy** | Identity through magical or world-stakes | World-building serves character arc |
| **SF** | Identity amid speculative futures | Extrapolation as metaphor for adolescence |
| **Thriller/Mystery** | Agency under threat | Protagonist must solve or survive through their own choices |
| **Horror** | Fear as identity test | Emotional stakes amplified by developmental vulnerability |
| **Romance** | First love, self-definition through relationship | HEA/HFN optional; growth required |
| **Historical** | Identity constrained by period and power | Period detail grounds emotional truth |

### TTS Considerations

**Narrator style**: Young without condescension, emotionally authentic
- Voice must sound young and credible — affect-y performance breaks trust immediately
- Dialogue-heavy genre: character voice differentiation is critical
- Emotional scenes: authenticity over polish; let the text land without overworking it
- Narrative pace: generally faster than adult literary fiction
- Never editorialize or soften — honor the character's experience as total

**Example persona prompt**:
> "Narrator in mid-to-late teens, or an adult who inhabits that register without performing it. Clear, direct, emotionally present. Pace is brisk but not rushed — these characters feel everything and think fast. Dialogue voices are distinct, especially friends vs. family vs. love interest. For emotional peaks: stay inside it, don't pull back. The irony is earned, not deployed to protect the reader."

### Common Word Counts

- Standalone: 50,000-80,000
- Upper YA / longer contemporary: up to 90,000
- Series entries: sometimes longer, series arc permitting

## TTS Production Quick Reference

| Genre | Pace | Emotional Range | Pauses | Character Voices |
|-------|------|-----------------|--------|------------------|
| Literary | Slow | Subtle | Long | Few, nuanced |
| Thriller | Variable (fast default) | Controlled tension | Short | Clear distinction |
| Romance | Medium | Wide | Medium (longer intimate) | Warm, distinct |
| Fantasy | Medium | Wide | Medium | Many, theatrical |
| SF | Medium | Precise | Medium | Technical comfort |
| Mystery | Measured | Sardonic to tense | Deliberate | Period-appropriate |
| Horror | Slow → fast | Dread to release | Long (tension) | Restrained |
| Memoir | Reflective | Authentic | Thoughtful | Author-match |
| Self-help | Clear | Encouraging | Instructional | Warm authority |
| YA | Variable | Wide emotional range | Medium | Essential character voices |
