# Revision Workflow

Systematic process for moving from draft to production-ready manuscript.

## Core Principle

**Big picture first, details last.** Fixing a typo in a scene you'll cut is wasted effort. Work from structure down to sentences.

## The Four Passes

| Pass | Focus | Questions | Tools |
|------|-------|-----------|-------|
| **1. Structure** | Does the story work? | Is the arc complete? Does tension build? Are promises paid off? | Framework overlay |
| **2. Scene** | Does each scene earn its place? | Goal-conflict-outcome? Does it advance plot AND character? | Scene cards |
| **3. Line** | Does the prose serve the story? | Is this the right word? Does rhythm match emotion? | Read aloud |
| **4. Polish** | Is it clean? | Typos, consistency, format | Spell check, style sheet |

---

## Pass 1: Structure (The Cruel Questions)

**Goal**: Identify what isn't working at the macro level.

### Process

1. **Summarize each chapter in one sentence.** If you can't, the chapter lacks focus.
2. **Map to framework.** Overlay your chosen structure (Three-Act, Save the Cat, Kishotenketsu). Where are you off-beat?
3. **Check promises.** List every setup. Confirm every payoff.
4. **Identify the actual story.** Sometimes the story you wrote isn't the story that wants to exist. Be honest.

### Red Flags

- Scenes that exist only for worldbuilding
- Characters who disappear for long stretches
- Stakes that decrease
- "And then" plotting instead of "therefore/but" plotting
- Protagonist who watches instead of acts

### Decisions at This Stage

- Cut chapters
- Reorder sequences
- Merge or split characters
- Change POV
- Alter the ending

**Do not proceed until structure is sound.** Everything else is decoration on a broken frame.

---

## Pass 2: Scene (The Diagnostic Questions)

**Goal**: Ensure every scene pulls weight.

### The Scene Checklist

For each scene, answer:

1. **Whose scene is it?** (POV character with the most at stake)
2. **What do they want?** (Scene goal—specific, concrete)
3. **What's in the way?** (Conflict—external, internal, or both)
4. **What's the outcome?** (Yes, No, Yes-but, No-and)
5. **What changed?** (If nothing changed, cut the scene)

### Scene-Level Fixes

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Scene feels flat | Raise the stakes or add obstacles |
| Scene feels rushed | Add sequel beats (reaction, dilemma, decision) |
| Scene feels slow | Cut sequel, enter later, leave earlier |
| Scene feels disconnected | Strengthen cause-effect chain to adjacent scenes |
| Dialogue feels false | Give each character a secret or agenda |

### Character Consistency Check

- Review each character's scenes in sequence
- Does their arc progress logically?
- Are voice markers consistent?
- Update the continuity log with any new facts established

## Pass 3: Line (The Craft Questions)

**Goal**: Make prose invisible—or, for literary work, intentional.

### Reading Aloud

**Mandatory.** Read every word out loud. Your ear catches what your eye misses:
- Awkward rhythms
- Repeated words
- Sentences that don't breathe
- Dialogue that feels written instead of spoken

### Prose Checklist

- **Verbs**: Is this the strongest verb? Does it need an adverb?
- **Dialogue tags**: Can "said" do the job? If not, is the action beat better?
- **Filtering**: Are we in the character's head or watching them think? ("She saw the door open" vs "The door opened")
- **Crutch words**: Search for your personal overuses (just, really, suddenly, felt, seemed)
- **Paragraph length**: Variation creates rhythm. Short paragraphs = fast. Long = slow.

### The 10% Cut

After line editing, find 10% to cut. This is always possible and always improves the work. Target:
- Redundant descriptions
- Dialogue that restates what action showed
- Scenes that overstay
- Adverbs doing work verbs should do

---

## Pass 4: Polish (The Production Questions)

**Goal**: Remove friction between reader and story.

### Consistency Check

Use the style sheet and continuity log:
- Character names spelled consistently?
- Eye colors, ages, locations stable?
- Timeline coherent?
- World rules followed?

### Format Check

- Chapter breaks in logical places
- Scene breaks clear
- Dialogue formatted correctly
- No orphan sentences or headers

### Fresh Eyes

At this stage, fresh readers catch what you're blind to:
- Beta readers for story-level response
- Copy editor for mechanical errors
- Proofreader for final typos

## Revision Logs

Maintain these documents throughout:

### Continuity Log

| Item | Detail | First Mentioned | Last Verified |
|------|--------|-----------------|---------------|
| Luna's size | "fit between two sunbeams" | Ch 1, p1 | Pass 2 |
| Flower color | purple | Ch 4, p35 | Pass 2 |

### Decision Log

Record major revision decisions and rationale. Future-you will want to know why.

| Date | Decision | Rationale |
|------|----------|-----------|
| 2026-01-26 | Cut bee's backstory | Slowed momentum, bee more effective as simple kindness |

### Style Sheet

| Category | Rule |
|----------|------|
| Spelling | Gray (not grey) |
| Numbers | Spell out one through nine |
| Dashes | Em dash with no spaces |

## When to Stop Revising

Revision can become infinite avoidance. Stop when:

1. Changes are lateral, not improvements
2. You're re-adding things you previously cut
3. Fresh readers have no structural concerns
4. You're editing out of fear rather than craft judgment

The goal is not perfection. The goal is a story that works.

## TTS Production Revision

Additional pass for audiobook production:

### Dialogue Attribution

- Is every speaker clear without "he said/she said"?
- Will TTS parser correctly identify speakers?
- Add explicit tags where ambiguous: `Luna said,` instead of `said the little cloud`

### Pronunciation Guides

Note unusual words for TTS:
- Character names with non-obvious pronunciation
- Made-up words
- Foreign language phrases

### Pause Markers

Insert `[PAGE TURN]` or `[BEAT]` markers where dramatic pauses needed.

### Voice Persona Review

- Does each character have enough dialogue to establish voice?
- Are character voice markers consistent (word choice, sentence structure)?
- Will switching voices between lines feel natural or jarring?
