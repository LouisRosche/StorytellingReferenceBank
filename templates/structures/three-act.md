# Three-Act Structure

The foundational Western narrative framework. Proportions: **25% Setup, 50% Confrontation, 25% Resolution**.

## Act One: Setup (0-25%)

Establishes world, protagonist, and status quo before disruption.

| Beat | Position | Purpose |
|------|----------|---------|
| Opening Image | 0-1% | "Before" snapshot—thematic visual of protagonist's starting state |
| Exposition | 1-10% | World, characters, ordinary life |
| Inciting Incident | ~12% | Disruption that demands response |
| Debate/Refusal | 12-25% | Protagonist resists call to action |
| Plot Point 1 | 25% | Protagonist commits—no turning back |

**Act One Questions:**
- What is the protagonist's ordinary world?
- What does the protagonist want (external goal)?
- What does the protagonist need (internal arc)?
- What event forces change?

## Act Two: Confrontation (25-75%)

Rising stakes, escalating conflict, character transformation.

| Beat | Position | Purpose |
|------|----------|---------|
| Entering New World | 25-30% | Protagonist navigates unfamiliar territory |
| Fun & Games | 30-50% | "Promise of the premise"—what audience came for |
| Midpoint | 50% | False victory OR false defeat; stakes raise |
| Complications | 50-65% | Antagonist gains ground, allies tested |
| All Is Lost | ~75% | Lowest point; something "dies" (literal or metaphorical) |
| Dark Night of Soul | 75% | Protagonist processes loss |

**Midpoint Types:**
- **False Victory**: Protagonist seems to win, but victory reveals deeper problem
- **False Defeat**: Seems like failure, but contains seeds of eventual success
- **Revelation**: Information that recontextualizes everything

## Act Three: Resolution (75-100%)

Synthesis and transformation.

| Beat | Position | Purpose |
|------|----------|---------|
| Plot Point 2 | 75-80% | New information or decision enables final push |
| Climax Build | 80-90% | Protagonist assembles resources, makes plan |
| Climax | 90-95% | Final confrontation; internal and external arcs converge |
| Resolution | 95-99% | New equilibrium established |
| Final Image | 99-100% | "After" snapshot—mirrors opening, shows transformation |

## Applying Three-Act to Different Lengths

| Format | Act 1 | Act 2 | Act 3 |
|--------|-------|-------|-------|
| 80K novel | ~20K words | ~40K words | ~20K words |
| 120-page screenplay | 30 pages | 60 pages | 30 pages |
| Short story (5K) | ~1,250 words | ~2,500 words | ~1,250 words |
| Picture book (500 words) | ~125 words | ~250 words | ~125 words |

## Common Problems

- **Saggy middle**: Act Two lacks clear midpoint shift; add revelation or reversal
- **Rushed ending**: Act Three compressed; ensure climax has breathing room
- **Passive protagonist**: Protagonist reacts but doesn't drive action; increase agency
- **Stakes unclear**: Audience doesn't understand what's at risk; clarify consequences
